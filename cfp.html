Open Up Digital Editions. Call for paper

In recent years, numerous innovative digital scholarly edition (DSE) projects have been implemented in Switzerland. The result is a growing scientific community.
Many questions though are still open and unresolved, especially with regard to the usability of digital editions and data : Do editions meet the research needs? Which standards should future projects adopt? How do digital editions remain accessible after the end of the project? How can edition projects be linked with each other?
These questions are of great concern to researchers, infrastructure providers and knowledge management institutions. The conference will provide a framework to discuss current approaches with a focus on developments in Switzerland and in relation to the international landscape.

Papers and posters on the following topics are welcome:

  • Sources and models of editions: critical, genetic, diplomatic, documentary, social, etc.
  • Minimal editions and prototypical workflows
  • Machine learning in scholarly editing
  • Editions and Linked Open Data
  • Editions and APIs
  • Long term preservation of data
  • Maintenance of interfaces by whom, where, until when and how
  • Linking, aggregations and search web services for interoperability
  • Use and reuse of editions in research and beyond
  • Digital scholarly editions in archives and libraries
  • Data stewardship, research support and digital scholarly editions

Type of submissions

Please submit an abstract of 800 to 1000 words for presenting a paper.
There is also the possibility of presenting a poster, both alone or in combination with a paper. Please submit an abstract of 400 to 500 words for presenting a poster only.
The abstract should be drafted according to the following templates:

  • template-paper-OpenUpDSEs.odt
  • template-paper-OpenUpDSEs.odt

Language

The digital scholarly editions community is becoming more and more international. For this reason, we propose to use English as lingua franca, but also accept papers in German, French and Italian. To facilitate the discussion, we encourage the use of slides in English in case the talk is in another language.

Submission process

The abstracts will be reviewed by the organisers. As the conference is meant to be a space of dialogue for anyone interested in digital scholarly editions, the abstracts won't undergo a strict selection process, but they will be collected in a book of abstracts.

For submitting an abstract, please visit the EasyChair submission page. Once logged in, you will be able to insert author information, title and keywords, and to choose the submission type (paper, poster, paper + poster).
